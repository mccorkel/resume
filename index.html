Other big areas of interest and startup ideas

  • Streaming Video with Portable Hardware/Real Time Streaming Protocols. Could we make a livestreamer hobbyist solution better than streaming from your phone and cheaper than a $3-10k streamer backpack? Could we get rid of the 5-10 second delay for streamers reacting to their large audience when they're in the middle of a city with 5G internet. Could we leverage data bonding to multiple edge network data centers or P2P WebRTC to enable 4K video?
  • Portable Healthcare Records/AI-Enabled Healthcare Records. Could we make it easier for you to get all of your health records from every doctor you've visited? Most doctors seem to already be a part of an HIE, but patients don't have access to it. What needs to change to give patients access to that? Could we make it easier for 3rd party app developers with aggregated patient data to keep an authorized data source attached to the health record so that doctors aren't hesitant to import data, so how do we solve this? TEFCA is coming which could help a lot with record portability, but it's probably going to be expensive- is there a way to improve it or lobby for laws/regulations to give cheaper and wider access?
  • Inequitable Access to AI-Accelerated Learning Programs with ESA's or Vouchers. A big problem with education vouchers seems to be that students who rely on support staff in public school because of disabilities or medical conditions, or who otherwise can't leave the public staff in public school because of disabilities or medical conditions, or who otherwise can't leave the public school system, don't have access to the same opportunities as other students who can leverage vouchers to attend private school or use it for programs like "2-Hour Learning". I would love to help figure out how to bridge this gap to give access for accelerated AI-learning programs to students even if they are blind, deaf, or suffer from a debilitating medical condition. There's a lot of pushback on the idea of ESA's across the country, and addressing these concerns to include these protected classes in future plans would make the concept much more palatable.
  • AI Boot Camp for Enterprise Coding. Make a "graduate" GauntletAI program focused specifically on the skills needed for an AI Engineer to operate in large corporate or enterprise environments. Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and others are investing billions of dollars in fine tuning models and creating tools used for massive codebases with thousands of user requests every second, but GauntletAI has barely touched on any of them like Poolside, Gemini Code Assist for Enterprise, and GitHub Copilot Pro. How do you blackbox and abstract massive interconnected webs of services, databases, and analytics? What is the best way to communicate standards to the LLM to maintain uniformity throughout your codebase? How do you have the LLM enforce security and mitigate risks to exposing new vulnerabilities with AI-generated code? How are previous assumptions around tech debt, product management, and adopting new tools and technology affected by moving towards an AI-First mindset? GauntletAI Graduate could also emphasize model choice, LLM evaluations, or machine learning best practices from a seasoned Data Scientist could be included.
